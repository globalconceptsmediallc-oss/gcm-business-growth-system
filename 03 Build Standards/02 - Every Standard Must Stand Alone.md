# Every Standard Must Stand Alone

Version
1.0

Status
Approved

Purpose

Every standard within the Global Concepts Media Business Growth System must be understandable on its own.

A reader should never be forced to understand multiple documents before applying one standard.

Each document should contain enough context to explain:

• Why it exists
• What problem it solves
• What is required
• How decisions are made
• How success is measured

The repository is an operating system—not a novel.

---

## Principle

Every standard must provide complete operational guidance without requiring another document to explain its meaning.

Standards may reference related documents for expansion, but never for basic understanding.

---

## Why It Matters

As organizations grow, documentation naturally becomes interconnected.

Without discipline, this creates documentation debt.

People begin asking:

"Which document explains this?"

"Where was that rule?"

"I know I've seen this somewhere."

Eventually knowledge becomes fragmented.

Fragmented knowledge creates inconsistent execution.

Stand-alone standards eliminate this problem.

Each document becomes independently useful while still contributing to the larger operating system.

This reduces onboarding time.

This improves consistency.

This makes continuous improvement significantly easier.

---

## Standard

Every standard shall contain enough information to answer:

What is this?

Why does it exist?

What is required?

How are decisions made?

How do we measure success?

How does this improve over time?

What other standards relate to this one?

No standard may rely on another document to explain its primary purpose.

Related documents may expand or specialize the topic but never replace the core explanation.

---

## Decision Framework

When creating or reviewing a standard, ask:

Can someone unfamiliar with the repository understand this document by itself?

Does the document explain why the rule exists?

Does it provide enough context to execute correctly?

Would removing every cross-reference still leave a usable operational document?

If the answer is no, the standard is incomplete.

---

## Measurement

A successful stand-alone standard demonstrates:

- Minimal clarification requests
- Consistent interpretation across team members
- Independent usability
- Clear decision guidance
- Reduced onboarding time
- Fewer conflicting implementations

If a reader frequently needs another document before using a standard, the document requires revision.

---

## Continuous Improvement

As the operating system expands, standards should become more self-contained—not less.

Future revisions should increase clarity, reduce ambiguity, simplify implementation, and eliminate unnecessary dependencies.

Cross-references should provide additional learning rather than required learning.

Every revision should move the repository closer to modular operational knowledge.
