# Standards Drive Decisions

Version

1.0

Status

Approved

Purpose

Every standard within the Global Concepts Media Business Growth System exists to improve decision-making.

The purpose of a standard is not simply to document a process.

Its purpose is to help people make better, faster, and more consistent decisions that produce measurable business growth.

When uncertainty exists, standards provide the decision framework that aligns actions with the principles of the organization.

---

## Principle

Good standards reduce the number of decisions that must be made from scratch.

Instead of relying on memory, assumptions, or personal preference, standards provide a repeatable framework for making high-quality decisions.

Every standard should make future decisions easier than previous ones.

---

## Why It Matters

Every business makes thousands of decisions.

Some are routine.

Some are strategic.

Some have significant financial consequences.

Without standards, decisions become dependent on:

- Individual experience.
- Personal opinion.
- Incomplete information.
- Inconsistent judgment.
- Tribal knowledge.
- Emotional reactions.

This creates variability, slows execution, and increases risk.

Standards create consistency.

They allow different people to make similar decisions because they are using the same decision framework.

The result is greater operational stability, higher quality outcomes, and more predictable business performance.

---

## Standard

Every standard shall provide clear guidance for decision-making.

A standard should answer:

- What decision is being made?
- What principles apply?
- What information should be considered?
- What criteria determine the best decision?
- What outcome is expected?

When multiple acceptable options exist, the standard should define how to evaluate trade-offs rather than prescribe a single answer.

Standards should reduce uncertainty without eliminating thoughtful judgment.

---

## Decision Framework

Before making a decision, ask:

Is there an existing standard that applies?

Does the proposed decision align with our First Principles?

Will the decision support measurable business growth?

Is there objective evidence supporting this choice?

Will the decision improve consistency across future work?

If no existing standard adequately addresses the situation, the decision should be documented and evaluated for inclusion as a future standard.

---

## Measurement

A decision-driven standard demonstrates:

- Faster decision-making.
- Greater consistency across team members.
- Reduced rework.
- Fewer repeated discussions.
- Better alignment with organizational principles.
- Improved business outcomes over time.

Repeated uncertainty surrounding the same decision indicates that the standard requires clarification or expansion.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Every important decision provides an opportunity to strengthen the operating system.

When new situations arise, the organization should determine whether the existing standard remains sufficient or whether the knowledge gained should become part of the standard.

The repository becomes more valuable as it captures better ways of making decisions.

Over time, fewer decisions rely solely on individual experience because organizational knowledge has become the default guide.
